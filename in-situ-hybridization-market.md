<p><p>In Situ Hybridization is a technique used to visualize specific DNA or RNA sequences within cells or tissues. Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH) uses fluorescent probes to detect target sequences, allowing for visualization under a fluorescence microscope. Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization utilizes chromogenic substrates to produce a colored reaction, making it visible under a brightfield microscope. Both techniques are valuable tools in molecular biology and can be used for various applications such as gene mapping, detecting genetic abnormalities, and studying gene expression.</p></p>
