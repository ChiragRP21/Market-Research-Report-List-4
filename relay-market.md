<p><p>Relays are electrical devices used to control high-power circuits with low-power signals. The market for relays can be classified into several types based on their operating principles. Electromechanical relays use mechanical contacts to switch the circuit on/off. Solid state relays use semiconductor technology instead of moving parts. Thermal relays are designed to protect electrical circuits from overheating. Other types of relays may include reed relays, hybrid relays, and polarized relays, each serving specific purposes in different applications.</p></p>
