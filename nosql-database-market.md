<p><p>NoSQL databases can be classified into four main types: Column, Document, Key-value, and Graph markets. Column databases store data in columns rather than rows, allowing for quick retrieval of specific data points. Document databases store data in flexible, JSON-like documents, making it easy to work with unstructured data. Key-value databases store data in key-value pairs, ideal for simple data structures. Graph databases use graph structures to represent and store data, making them perfect for complex relationships and networked data.</p></p>
