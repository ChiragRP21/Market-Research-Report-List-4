<p><p>Reverse shoulder arthroplasty is a surgical procedure that is used to treat patients with severe shoulder arthritis, rotator cuff tears, or other shoulder conditions that cannot be effectively treated through traditional shoulder replacement surgery. In this procedure, the normal ball-and-socket structure of the shoulder joint is reversed, with a metal ball attached to the shoulder socket and a plastic socket attached to the upper arm bone. This allows the deltoid muscle to take over the function of the damaged rotator cuff, providing improved stability and range of motion for the patient.</p><p>The Reverse Shoulder Arthroplasty Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.6% during the forecast period.
