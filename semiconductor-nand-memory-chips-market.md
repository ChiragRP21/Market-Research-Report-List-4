<p><ul><li>SLC</li><li>MLC</li><li>TLC</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Semiconductor NAND memory chips come in three main types: SLC (Single Level Cell), MLC (Multi Level Cell), and TLC (Triple Level Cell). SLC chips store one bit of data per cell, making them the fastest and most durable but also the most expensive. MLC chips store multiple bits per cell, offering a balance between speed, durability, and cost. TLC chips store three bits per cell, providing the highest storage capacity but at the cost of slower performance and reduced lifespan.</p></p>
