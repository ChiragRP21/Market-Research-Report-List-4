Medium-heat nonfat dried milk undergoes slightly higher temperatures during processing, striking a balance between flavor retention and microbial safety. High-heat nonfat dried milk is processed at higher temperatures to ensure maximum microbial safety but may result in a slightly cooked flavor and lower nutritional value. Each type caters to different consumer preferences and industry requirements.</p></p>
