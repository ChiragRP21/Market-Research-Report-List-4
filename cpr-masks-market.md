<p><p>CPR masks are available in several different types, including facial, laryngeal, oral, and nasal pillow masks. Facial masks cover the mouth and nose, providing a seal for delivering breaths during CPR. Laryngeal masks are inserted into the throat to maintain an open airway. Oral masks are placed over the mouth, while nasal pillow masks cover the nostrils. Each type of CPR mask serves a specific purpose in providing effective ventilation and oxygen delivery during resuscitation efforts.</p></p>
