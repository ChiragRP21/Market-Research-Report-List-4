<p><p>Rail heaters are essential for preventing ice buildup on rail tracks during cold weather. There are three main types of rail heating systems: electric heating, gas heating, and steam heating. Electric heating systems use electric currents to generate heat, while gas heating systems use combustion of natural gas or propane. Steam heating systems rely on steam pipes running along the tracks to melt ice. Each type of rail heater has its own advantages and limitations, depending on factors such as cost, efficiency, and environmental impact.</p></p>
