<p><p>Access Control Terminals are categorized into three types based on the method of control they use. Discretionary Access Control (DAC) Terminals allow users to determine who has access to their resources. Mandatory Access Control (MAC) Terminals restrict access based on set rules and policies. Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) Terminals assign access rights based on a user's role within an organization. Each type offers a different level of control and security, catering to various security needs of organizations.</p></p>
