<p><p>Railway batteries are essential for providing backup power to trains and other railway systems. The main types of railway batteries include Lead-Acid, Li-Ion (Lithium-Ion), and Ni-Cd (Nickel-Cadmium). Lead-Acid batteries are commonly used for their affordability and reliability. Li-Ion batteries offer higher energy density and longer lifespan, making them ideal for modern railway systems. Ni-Cd batteries are known for their robustness and ability to withstand extreme conditions. Other types of railway batteries may include advanced technologies like solid-state batteries or hydrogen fuel cells. Each type of battery has its own advantages and is chosen based on specific requirements and applications in the railway industry.</p></p>
