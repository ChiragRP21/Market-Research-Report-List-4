<p><p>Enhanced fire protection systems are classified into two main types: active systems and passive systems. Active systems involve the use of equipment like fire alarms, sprinkler systems, and fire extinguishers that actively respond to a fire incident. Passive systems, on the other hand, are built into the structure of a building and work to contain or suppress fires, such as fire-resistant walls, doors, and floors. Both types of systems work together to provide comprehensive fire protection for buildings and structures.</p></p>
