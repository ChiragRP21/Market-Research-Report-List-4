Current Mode PWM controllers focus on controlling the current flowing through the system, ensuring stability and efficiency in high-power applications. On the other hand, Voltage Mode PWM controllers primarily regulate the output voltage, providing accurate voltage regulation in low-power applications. Both types of controllers offer distinct advantages based on the specific requirements of the system, making them crucial in various industries such as automotive, telecommunications, and aerospace.</p></p>
